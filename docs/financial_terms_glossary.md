## üìä Market Instruments

### ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A collection of securities (stocks, bonds, commodities) that trades on an exchange like a single stock. Offers instant diversification at lower costs than buying individual stocks.

**Example:** SPY holds all 500 companies in the S&P 500.

### SPY (SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust)
The largest and most liquid ETF that tracks the S&P 500 index. When you buy one share of SPY, you own a tiny piece of 500 major U.S. companies.

**Ticker Symbol:** SPY  
**Launched:** 1993  
**Typical Use:** Benchmark for U.S. stock market performance

### S&P 500 Index
A market-capitalization-weighted index of 500 large U.S. publicly traded companies. Represents ~80% of the total U.S. stock market value.

**Key Companies:** Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Tesla

## üíπ Price Metrics

### Closing Price
The final price at which a security trades when the market closes for the day.

**U.S. Market Hours:** 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET  
**Why Important:** Used as the official daily price for calculations and reporting

### Trading Days
Days when stock exchanges are open for business. Excludes weekends and market holidays.

**Annual Count:** ~252 trading days (vs. 365 calendar days)  
**Market Holidays:** New Year's, MLK Day, Presidents' Day, Good Friday, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas

### Volume
The number of shares traded during a specific period. High volume indicates strong interest or significant news.

**Low Volume:** < 1M shares/day (for most stocks)  
**High Volume:** > 10M shares/day

## üìà Technical Indicators

### Moving Average (MA)
The average price over a specified number of periods, recalculated as new data arrives. Smooths out short-term volatility to reveal trends.

**Formula:** Sum of closing prices √∑ Number of periods

**Example (3-day MA):**
```
Day 1: $100
Day 2: $102
Day 3: $101
3-day MA = (100 + 102 + 101) √∑ 3 = $101
```

### 50-Day Moving Average
Short-term trend indicator showing the average closing price over the last 50 trading days (~2.5 months).

**Characteristics:**
- Reacts quickly to price changes
- Used to identify short-term momentum
- More volatile than long-term averages

### 200-Day Moving Average
Long-term trend indicator showing the average closing price over the last 200 trading days (~10 months).

**Characteristics:**
- Slower to react, filters out noise
- Represents major market sentiment
- Strong support/resistance level

### Support Level
A price level where a stock tends to stop falling and bounce back up. Think of it as a "floor."

### Resistance Level
A price level where a stock tends to stop rising and fall back down. Think of it as a "ceiling."

## üéØ Trading Signals

### Golden Cross
A bullish signal that occurs when a short-term moving average (typically 50-day) crosses above a long-term moving average (typically 200-day).

**Interpretation:** Suggests upward momentum is building  
**Historical Accuracy:** ~70% success rate in predicting bull markets  
**Timeframe:** Signals can last weeks to months

**Visual:**
```
Price trend moving up ‚Üó
50-day MA crosses ABOVE 200-day MA
= Potential buying opportunity
```

### Death Cross
The opposite of a Golden Cross - when the 50-day MA crosses below the 200-day MA.

**Interpretation:** Bearish signal suggesting potential decline  
**Action:** Consider reducing positions or hedging

## üêÇüêª Market Conditions

### Bull Market
A sustained period (typically months to years) where stock prices rise or are expected to rise by 20% or more from recent lows.

**Characteristics:**
- Investor confidence is high
- Economic indicators are positive
- Trading volume increases
- "Buy the dip" mentality

**Average Duration:** 3-5 years

### Bear Market
A sustained decline of 20% or more from recent highs, typically lasting several months.

**Characteristics:**
- Investor pessimism
- Declining economic indicators
- Lower trading volume
- "Sell on rallies" mentality

**Average Duration:** 9-18 months

### Correction
A temporary decline of 10-20% from recent highs. Considered healthy market behavior.

**Duration:** Typically days to weeks  
**Frequency:** 1-2 times per year on average

## üìä Analysis Methods

### Technical Analysis
Using historical price and volume data to predict future price movements. Focuses on charts and patterns rather than company fundamentals.

**Tools:** Moving averages, RSI, MACD, candlestick patterns

### Fundamental Analysis
Evaluating a company's financial health, earnings, revenue, and market position to determine its true value.

**Metrics:** P/E ratio, earnings per share, revenue growth, debt levels

### Quantitative Analysis
Using mathematical and statistical models to analyze securities and make trading decisions.

**Applications:** Algorithmic trading, risk management, portfolio optimization

## üî¢ Statistical Concepts

### Volatility
The degree of variation in a security's price over time. Higher volatility means larger price swings.

**Low Volatility:** Stable, predictable (e.g., utility stocks)  
**High Volatility:** Unpredictable, risky (e.g., tech startups, crypto)

### Trend
The general direction in which a security's price is moving.

- **Uptrend:** Higher highs and higher lows
- **Downtrend:** Lower highs and lower lows
- **Sideways:** No clear direction

### Momentum
The rate of acceleration in price changes. Strong momentum means prices are moving quickly in one direction.

**Indicators:** RSI, MACD, Stochastic Oscillator

## üíº Investment Strategies

### Buy and Hold
Long-term strategy of purchasing securities and holding them regardless of market fluctuations.

**Pros:** Lower fees, tax advantages, compound growth  
**Cons:** Requires patience, risk of major downturns

### Day Trading
Buying and selling securities within the same trading day to profit from short-term price movements.

**Pros:** No overnight risk  
**Cons:** High stress, requires significant time and capital

### Swing Trading
Holding positions for several days to weeks to capture short-to-medium-term gains.

**Timeframe:** 2 days to 6 weeks  
**Focus:** Technical patterns and momentum

---

## üé≤ Risk Metrics

### Risk
The possibility of losing some or all of an investment. Different types include market risk, liquidity risk, and company-specific risk.

### Diversification
Spreading investments across different assets, sectors, or geographies to reduce risk.

**Example:** Instead of buying only tech stocks, own tech + healthcare + energy + bonds

### Portfolio
A collection of financial investments like stocks, bonds, ETFs, and cash.
